PORK CHOPS WITH YAMS AND APPLE CIDER
Recipe By : Bon Appetit Serving Size : 6 Preparation Time : 0:00 Categories : Pork Chops
Amount Measure Ingredient Preparation -------------- ------------ -------------- ----------------
1 1/2 Tablespoons olive oil 5 Ounces center-cut pork chops, (six) 3 large onions, sliced 3 large yams (red skinned) Peeled cut 3/4 thick rounds 6 Tablespoons butter 1 Tablespoon rosemary, fresh, chopped or 1 1/2 Teaspoons rosemary, dried
1/4 Cup flour 1 Teaspoon salt 2 Cups apple cider 14 1/2 Ounces chicken broth, low-salt
Preheat oven to 350 degrees. heat olive oil in heavy large skillet over high heat. Sprinkle pork chops with salt and pepper. Add pork chops to skillet and brown well, about 2 minutes per side. Using tongs, transfer pork chops to plate. Add onions to same skillet and saute until tender and golden, about 10 minutes. Arrange onions in bottom of large roasting pan (do not clean skillet). Top onions with sliced yams. Melt butter in same skillet over medium heat. Drizzle half of melted butter over yams. Sprinkle with rosemary. Top with pork chops in single layer. Add flour and salt to remaining butter in skillet. Stir over medium heat 1 minute. Gradually mix in cider and broth. Bring to boil, stirring constantly. Boil 2 minutes, stirring frequently. Pour over chops.
Cover pan tightly with foil. Bake until pork is tender and juices thicken, about 1 hour. Transfer to platter.
