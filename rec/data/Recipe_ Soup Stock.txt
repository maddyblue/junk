SOUP STOCK
Recipe By : Serving Size : 1 Preparation Time : 0:00 Categories : Soups
Amount Measure Ingredient Preparation -------------- ------------ -------------- ----------------
6 Pounds meaty beef soup bones or 4 Pounds bony chicken pieces 3 carrots, cut up 1 large onion, sliced 2 stalks celery, cut up 1 large tomato 1 small cabbage, cut up 8 Whole black peppercorns 4 sprigs parsley 1 bay leaf 2 Teaspoons salt 2 Teaspoons dried thyme, crushed 1 clove garlic, crushed
1/4 Cup water, cold 1 egg
1. In a large shallow roasting pan place soup bones, carrots, and onion. Bake bones, uncovered, in a 450 degree oven 30 minutes or till bones are well browned, turning occasionally.
2. Drain off fat. In a 10 quart Dutch oven or kettle place the browned bones, carrots, and onion.
3. Pour 1/2cup water into roasting pan and rinse. Pour this liquid into Dutch oven.
4. Add 12 cups water, celery, tomato, cabbage, peppercorns, parsley, bay leaf, salt, thyme, and garlic.
5. Bring mixture to boiling. Reduce heat. Cover; simmer 5 hours.
6. Strain stock, by ladling it through a sieve lined with 1 or 2 layers of cheesecloth; discard meat, bvegetables and seasonings.
7. Clarify stock, if desired. To clarify, separate egg, crushing and reserving the shell. Stir together the 1/4 cup cold water, the egg white, and the crushed egg shell. Add to stock; bring to boiling. Remove from heat; let stock stand 5 minutes.
8. Strain clarifed stock again through a sieve lined with 1 or 2 layers of cheesecloth.
9. If using stock while hot, skim fat using a metal spoon. Or, ladle the stock into pint or quart jars; cover and refrigerate to chill quickly; lift off fat witha fork. Stock may be stored in refrigerator abotu 3 days, or in freezer up to 6 months. Label type of stock, the quantity, and date. Makes about 8 cups.
